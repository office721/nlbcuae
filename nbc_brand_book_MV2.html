Who we are

A council twenty-five years in the making.

The Netherlands Business Council UAE has connected Dutch enterprise with the United Arab Emirates since 2000. We are a non-profit, member-led organisation working alongside the Dutch Consul General in Dubai and Dubai Chambers.

Our members include some of the most recognised Dutch companies operating in the region, Boskalis, FrieslandCampina, ING, KLM, Philips, Shell, Unilever, Van Oord, alongside hundreds of small and medium businesses building their UAE presence.

This document is the codification of how that twenty-five-year-old council looks, sounds, and behaves on every surface where it appears.

We connect people and knowledge between two business cultures.
The council, in one sentence
02
Vision

Where we are going.

A future where Dutch enterprise and the United Arab Emirates operate as one connected economic community, sharing knowledge, talent, and trust across two continents.

The vision is not a finish line. It is the direction we point every event, every introduction, every advocacy conversation, and every member service in. If a decision moves us closer to that future, it is the right decision. If it does not, we reconsider.

03
Mission

What we do, every day.

We make doing business between the Netherlands and the UAE easier, faster, and more successful, for our members and the wider Dutch business community.

We do this through four concrete activities:

  1. Convening members through events, networking, and dedicated working groups.
  2. Sharing market knowledge through publications, government briefings, and business insights.
  3. Representing Dutch business interests to UAE authorities, Dutch government, and partner councils.
  4. Recognising excellence through our Business Awards and member spotlights.

Tone of voice

Dutch directness, Emirati hospitality.

The NBC voice carries two cultural heritages at once. We say what we mean, but we say it warmly. We respect the time and intelligence of every reader, and we do not waste either.

We are

Not arrogant
Knowledgeable
Not academic
Bilingual in spirit
Not stiff in translation

We avoid

  • Corporate jargon like synergise, leverage, best-in-class, world-class, premier, leading.
  • Empty superlatives. If we cannot back it with a number or a name, we do not write it.
  • Bureaucratic formality. We are a business council, not a ministry.
  • Emoji in formal communications. Used sparingly on social, never in newsletters or member documents.

Voice in practice

The same invitation, written the wrong way and the right way.

Avoid
We are pleased to cordially invite you to our exclusive premier networking event taking place at a venue to be confirmed.
Write
Join us at Le Petit Belge on 14 May, 7pm. Twelve other Dutch businesses, drinks, and a working dinner.
Avoid
NBC offers members access to a world-class network of leading business executives across the Emirates.
Write
Two hundred Dutch business owners, founders, and country heads, all already operating in the UAE.
Avoid
We endeavour to facilitate strategic engagement opportunities to leverage cross-border synergies.
Write
We introduce you to the right people. Then we get out of the way.

07
Construction & rationale

Two heritages, one mark.

The NBC mark is not arbitrary. Every formal decision in it carries meaning. Understanding the rationale helps every designer, partner, and member apply the brand with confidence.

Reference one, the UAE Nation Brand.

The forward lean, the parallel rhythm, and the staggered ascent deliberately echo the visual language of the UAE Nation Brand mark, the four-stripe logo used by the United Arab Emirates across government, tourism, and official national communications.

This is intentional citizenship. By sharing a formal vocabulary with the UAE national mark, NBC visually places itself inside the family of identities that belong to the Emirates, not as a foreign organisation operating here, but as an institution that has made the UAE its second home. Twenty-five years of presence earns that right.

UAE Nation Brand · reference

Reference two, Rembrandt's etchings.

The parallel-line treatment also recalls Rembrandt's etching technique. The seventeenth-century Dutch master built his most famous works, the Self-Portrait Leaning on a Stone Sill, The Hundred Guilder Print, on dense parallel hatching that constructs form, light, and depth from nothing but repeated lines.

NBC's three bars are an abstraction of that hatching, a modernist reduction of a 400-year-old Dutch artistic vocabulary into a contemporary geometric mark.

Two reference points, one shape. The same three forward-leaning bars carry a fifty-year-old UAE national symbol and a four-hundred-year-old Dutch art tradition simultaneously. That is the bridge NBC builds.

Why orange.

The bars use NBC Orange (#FF9B00), which sits in the same family as the official Dutch national orange of the House of Orange-Nassau, the Dutch royal lineage that gives the country its other identity (and its football kit). It is unambiguously, unmistakably Dutch, without being literal flag-replication. It also reads cleanly at any size, from social avatar to building signage.

Why the corner detail.

Each bar carries a subtle rounding on its top-left and bottom-right corners, mirroring the corner treatment of the UAE Nation Brand bars. The top-right and bottom-left corners remain sharp. The result is a leaf-like or blade-like shape that adds craft without adding ornamentation.

Logo variants

One mark, four treatments.

A real brand needs to live on every surface, light, dark, single-colour, photo backgrounds. These four variants cover every legitimate use case.

Primary, on light
The default. Letterheads, web headers, documents, member materials.
Reversed, on dark
Event banners, slide decks with dark themes, social tiles, signage.
Monochrome, on light
Single-colour print, embossing, faxes, legal documents, archival.
Monochrome reversed
Embroidery on dark fabric, etched signage, video lower-thirds.

Icon-only mark

For social avatars, app icons, favicons, and any context where the wordmark is illegible or redundant. Never use the icon-only mark in formal documents where the full name has not appeared elsewhere on the page.

Member badge

For our members to display.

A dedicated version of the NBC mark, with the caption “Member of” set above the wordmark, is supplied to members for use on their websites, email signatures, signage, and printed materials. It signals affiliation without claiming representation.

When to use it

The Member Badge is the only NBC mark that members are licensed to display on their own surfaces. The plain primary lockup is reserved for NBC’s own communications. The badge tells the viewer two things in one image: the company is a member of NBC, and NBC stands behind that membership.

Stacked badge

Horizontal badge

The horizontal version suits website footers, email signatures, and narrow header strips, anywhere the stacked version would feel oversized.

Caption type

The “Member of” text uses the brand’s standard caption style: Geist Medium, set in uppercase at 11 px with 3 px letter-spacing, in neutral grey (#8B8B8B). It is intentionally smaller than the main wordmark so that it qualifies the NBC mark without competing with it.

Who gets the badge. The Member Badge is supplied to current paying members only and may be displayed for the duration of active membership. On lapse or termination of membership, the badge must be removed from all surfaces. Non-members may not use the badge for any purpose.

Member badge rules

  • Use only the supplied stacked or horizontal version. Do not recreate or redraw.
  • Do not change the “Member of” wording or its placement.
  • Do not place the badge alongside competitor logos in a way that suggests endorsement.
  • Do not use the badge to imply official NBC representation in any commercial transaction.
  • All other logo rules (clear space, minimum size, do-nots) apply to the Member Badge as written.

Clear space & sizing

Give it room to breathe.

The NBC mark works because of its restraint. Crowding it with adjacent elements eats that restraint and weakens the brand. The clear space and minimum size rules below are non-negotiable.

Clear space

The shaded zone below is the “no-go area” around the logo. No other element, text, image, button, photo, or edge of page, may enter this zone. The minimum gap on every side equals the width of one orange bar.

Minimum sizes

Below these sizes, the bars and wordmark lose legibility. Use the icon-only mark for smaller applications.

  • Print, primary lockup: 25 mm wide minimum.
  • Print, horizontal lockup: 35 mm wide minimum.
  • Digital, primary lockup: 120 px wide minimum.
  • Digital, horizontal lockup: 180 px wide minimum.
  • Digital, icon-only: 32 px wide minimum.

What not to do

Eight things to never do.

When in doubt, treat the logo with restraint. If a transformation is not listed in this brand book, it is not approved.

Do not stretch or distort.
Do not change the bar colour.
Do not rotate the mark.
Do not add shadows or effects.
Do not place on busy backgrounds.
Do not recolour individual bars.
Do not outline or hollow out.
Do not change the wordmark font.

Accessibility

NBC Blue (#22478B) on white meets WCAG AAA contrast for body text. NBC Orange (#FF9B00) on white does not meet WCAG AA for body text and should be used only for headlines, accents, or the brand mark itself, never for paragraph text.

For dark backgrounds, white text on NBC Blue meets WCAG AAA. White text on NBC Orange falls below WCAG AA and is reserved for short labels or the wordmark.

If in doubt, less colour. Restrained palette use is more recognisable than maximalist colour. A page with one orange accent reads as NBC. A page covered in orange reads as an advertisement.

Typography

One workhorse, one editorial accent.

A single primary typeface carries the entire brand, headlines, body, captions, the wordmark. A second face appears only for editorial moments, pull quotes, ceremonial section openers, the occasional italic word.

Primary, Geist

An open-source, modern, geometric sans-serif designed by Vercel. Distinctive without being expressive. Free for commercial use. Available from Google Fonts and the Geist GitHub repository.
